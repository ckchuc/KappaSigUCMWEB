F.A.Q

Will I Be Hazed?

No, under no circumstances will you be hazed. You should never participate in any activity that you feel uncomfortable with. Under UC Merced and the Kappa Sigma Fraternity, hazing will not be tolerated and should be reported immediately to the Fraternity and Sorority Council on campus as well as Kappa Sigma Headquaters.

How will recruitment affect my grades?

With Scholarship being one of the four pillars of Kappa Sigma, we take our education fairly seriously and with high regard. Grades tend to usually drop during the semester you are pledging due to pledging being like an extra 4 unit course. However, do not fear, we have brothers within the fraternity who are more than willing to help you with any courses you are taking. We have brothers across all majors with past material that might prove to be helpful. In addition, the fraternity also offers support in time management, tutoring, as well as other incentives to boost your GPA.

What will you get out of joining Kappa Sigma?

The Kappa Sigma fraternity offers you a chance of lifelong brothers, endless leadership opportunities, social and sports activies, academic support and various networking opportunities with brothers and alumni.

What are the total costs to become a member of Kappa Sigma?

During initiation, dues are $320 per person, however for every semester after that, it will be $400. All the money goes towards social events that we throw with other fraternities and sororities on campus. It might seem a lot at first, but the total sum of $400 can be split up into payment plans which can be worked out with our active treasurer.

Do you guys have a fraternity house?

Currently, we do not have a fraternity house as do other fraternities and sororities that are affiliated with UC Merced.

What is a "Bid"?

Bid is an invitation to membership into the fraternity. It is the piece of paper from our chapter that will cordially invite you to join the organization.

What are the requirements to participate in recruitment?

  • Have earned a minimum of 12 units as a full-time student in a post-secondary institution after high school graduation.
  • Have at least a 2.5 cumulative GPA.
  • Be enrolled in a minimum of 12 credit hours.
  • Be in good standing with the university (academic and conduct)
  • Transfer students who have earned less than 12 University of California credits need to provide a transcript as verification of GPA eligibility.
